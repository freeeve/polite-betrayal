## Analysis

**Overall win rate: 34.7%** (vs 28% baseline Experiment C)

This is a significant improvement of +6.7 percentage points over the baseline (0.6*ply1 + 0.4*ply3).

Adding ply2 (opponent response evaluation) to the blend provides additional signal about positions
that are vulnerable to enemy counterattacks. The 0.2 weight on ply2 helps penalize positions
that look good in ply1 but collapse when opponents respond.

### Per-power comparison with baseline (Experiment C = 28% overall)

The blend-all approach shows strong performance across most powers, with Turkey (65%) and
France (61%) as the best performers, and Austria (8%) and Russia (7%) remaining weak.

### Runtime

Total: ~68 minutes (4090s) for 700 games. Similar to Experiment C since the 3-ply structure
is the same; the only change is including ply2 in the score calculation (which was already computed).
