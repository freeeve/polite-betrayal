## Analysis

Adding RM+ search (5-second time budget, 80% neural weight) to the gonnx policy network did NOT produce any wins against medium opponents. The overall win rate remains 0%.

However, there are notable SC trajectory improvements compared to the greedy baseline:

- **France** improved from 0.1 to 2.2 avg final SCs, with P95 reaching 13-15 SCs in mid-game (1908-1911). This represents a massive improvement in mid-game strength, but France still couldn't close out victories before being overwhelmed by 6 medium opponents.
- **Turkey** improved from 0.0 to 2.3 avg final SCs, maintaining 4+ SCs through 1908 with P95 reaching 10 SCs.
- **England** improved from 1.0 to 1.6 avg final SCs.
- **Germany** improved from 5.2 peak (1902) to 5.2 peak but collapsed to 0 by 1914 in both cases.
- **Russia** improved from 0.0 to 0.3 avg final SCs, with 2 draws (vs 1 in baseline).

The search makes the gonnx bot significantly stronger in the mid-game (building larger empires) but unable to convert that advantage into wins against 6 coordinated medium opponents. The 5s time budget produces ~130s/game (2.8x slower than greedy) but the win rate improvement is zero.

Key issues:
- The policy network's order quality may be the bottleneck, not search depth
- 6 medium opponents coordinate implicitly against the strongest player
- The gonnx retreat and build phases still use greedy policy (no search), which may contribute to late-game collapse
